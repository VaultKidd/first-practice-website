The History of Humanity

Travel from the dawn of humanity's awakening, through the rise and fall of civilizations to the modern day

Prehistoric

Humans or Homospaiens first arose around 300,000 years ago in Africa. Over the next 100-125 thousand years, humans would wonder around the Earth and settle in tribes across the continents as we know them today. Early humans eventually learned to make tools out of rocks and stones such as axes, spears and knives, and began hunting things which in the past, used to hunt them.

Due to the new food available to humans through hunting and gathering, we learned to use our brains more efficiently and started developing things such as agriculture. Through agriculture and irrigation, humans now had to worry less about daily survival, and more about learning new things to expand our understanding of the world. Agriculture eventually led to the formation of small villages, and these small villages ended up being assimilated into towns, which then needed someone to rule over them.

Civilization

Civilization as we know it first came around 3,000 years ago with the rise of the Sumer in Mesapotamia. Which then spanned a whole conglomerate of people following suit.

Over the next 5,000 years civilisations would rise and fall, with many notable ones being the Ancient Egyptians, Indus Valley, China, Ancient Greeks, Romans, Byzantines and many many more up until the modern day. Civilisations would battle with each other over land, resources and pride, and due to the decisions of many before us, we are at this point today, with the technology and knowledge all thanks to the civilisations and empires before us.

Modern Day

Modern day has placed us in the information age. Media and the internet allows us to access million of pieces of information which has been collected throughout ancient history as well as new information created every single day. This age is seeing us accelerate faster and faster into the unknown, and with many modern bright minds driving us towards even greater cultural and technological development, the world ahead of us is whatever we decide to make it, as long as we don't drive ourselves to extinction, as many advanced and ingenious civilizations before us, have.
